# Custom Marathon Medals: What to Look for in a Quality Supplier

Selecting the right custom marathon medals for your event requires understanding material properties, design options, and quality indicators. This guide helps race directors evaluate suppliers and make informed purchasing decisions for their running events.

## Why Zinc Alloy Is the Top Choice for Marathon Medals

Zinc alloy is the preferred material for marathon medals because it combines lightweight feel with excellent durability and fine detail reproduction. With a density of approximately 6.6 g/cm³, zinc alloy is significantly lighter than brass (8.4 g/cm³) or iron (7.8 g/cm³), which means participants can wear their medals comfortably throughout the post-race celebration without neck strain. The die-casting process used for zinc alloy medals achieves tolerances of ±0.1 mm, which means intricate race logos and text remain sharp and readable even on medals as small as 50 mm in diameter. Additionally, zinc alloy naturally resists corrosion, which means your medals maintain their gold finish without tarnishing, even when stored in varying humidity conditions.

## Key Features of High-Quality Custom Running Medals

When evaluating custom running medals, several features distinguish premium products from basic alternatives.

### Scratch-Resistant Finish
A quality scratch-resistant finish uses a protective clear coat applied after plating. This coating measures between 10-20 microns in thickness, which means the gold surface resists scuffs from handling and stacking during distribution. For event organizers managing thousands of medals, this durability ensures each medal looks pristine when handed to finishers.

### Custom Engraving Capabilities
Precision engraving creates recessed text and logos with a depth of 0.3-0.5 mm, which means your event name, date, and sponsor logos remain clearly legible even after years of display. Laser engraving achieves line widths as fine as 0.1 mm, which means detailed artwork like course maps or runner silhouettes reproduces faithfully on the medal surface.

### Ribbon Quality and Attachment
Included ribbons use 38 mm wide grosgrain polyester with heat-sealed edges, which means they resist fraying during the event and maintain their color through multiple wears. The ribbon attachment point uses a reinforced loop or metal claw, which means the ribbon stays securely connected even when medals are handled by excited finishers.

## How to Evaluate Medal Quality Before Placing a Bulk Order

Follow this checklist when assessing samples from a marathon medal supplier:

1. **Inspect surface finish under direct light** — Hold the medal at a 45-degree angle to a desk lamp. A quality gold finish shows uniform color without streaks, bubbles, or dull patches. This indicates proper electroplating with at least 0.5 microns of gold layer.

2. **Run your fingernail across engraved areas** — The edges of engraved text should feel smooth, not sharp. Rough edges suggest poor deburring after die-casting, which means the medal may scratch other surfaces during storage.

3. **Test the ribbon attachment** — Pull the ribbon firmly away from the medal body. The attachment point should hold without bending or loosening. A reinforced attachment withstands at least 5 kg of pull force, which means it won't fail during distribution.

4. **Check weight consistency** — Weigh multiple samples on a digital scale. All medals from the same production run should fall within ±1 gram of each other. This confirms consistent die-casting quality, which means every participant receives the same experience.

5. **Examine the back surface** — The reverse side should be smooth and free of tool marks or uneven texture. A clean back provides a proper surface for engraving event details, which means your second-side design looks professional.

## Designing Your Marathon Medal: Tips for a Memorable Finish Line

### Choose an Appropriate Shape and Size
Standard marathon medals range from 60-80 mm in diameter. A 70 mm round medal provides ample surface area for detailed artwork, which means your course logo and sponsor names fit without overcrowding. For half-marathons, consider a 60 mm medal, which means the size remains proportional to the achievement while keeping production costs efficient.

### Optimize Logo Placement
Position your primary event logo in the center of the medal face, where the die-casting detail is sharpest. Place sponsor logos along the outer edge or on the reverse side, which means the main design remains clean while still recognizing partners. Keep all text at least 3 mm from the medal edge, which means it won't be cut off during production.

### Select Colors and Finish
Gold remains the most popular finish for marathon medals, with approximately 70% of running events choosing this color according to industry surveys. Pair the gold base with enamel accents in your event colors — red, blue, or green enamels add visual contrast, which means the medal stands out against the ribbon. Avoid dark enamels on gold backgrounds, as they may reduce legibility of engraved details.

## Ensuring Your Medals Meet Event Standards

A reliable marathon medal supplier maintains consistent quality across every medal in your order. Industry-standard production tolerances for die-cast medals include dimensional accuracy of ±0.2 mm, which means all medals in a 5,000-unit batch have identical size and shape. Surface finish uniformity should show no visible color variation between medals, which means every finisher receives a medal that looks identical to the sample you approved.

Ribbon quality is equally important — polyester grosgrain ribbons with heat-sealed edges maintain their appearance after repeated handling. The ribbon attachment system should be tested to ensure it holds securely, as loose ribbons create a poor participant experience. When evaluating suppliers, request a pre-production sample that matches your final specifications, as this sample becomes your quality benchmark for the entire production run.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Marathon Medals

### What is the best material for custom marathon medals?
Zinc alloy is the preferred material for custom marathon medals due to its lightweight feel (approximately 40% lighter than brass), scratch-resistant finish, and ability to hold detailed engravings with tolerances of ±0.1 mm. This combination makes it ideal for events where participants will wear their medals for extended periods.

### Can I add my event logo to a marathon medal?
Yes, custom engraving allows you to add logos, text, and designs directly onto the medal surface. Laser engraving achieves line widths as fine as 0.1 mm, which means even complex artwork like sponsor logos or course maps reproduces clearly. You can place logos on both the front and back of the medal.

### How do custom marathon medals compare to stock options?
Custom marathon medals offer unique designs and branding, while stock medals are pre-made and less flexible. Custom options let you control shape, size, color, and engraving details, which means your event has a distinctive finisher award. Stock medals are available immediately but lack personalization.

### What features should I look for in a marathon medal?
Look for a lightweight design (zinc alloy recommended), scratch-resistant finish with a protective clear coat, secure ribbon attachment that withstands at least 5 kg of pull force, and clear engraving with recessed details at least 0.3 mm deep. These features ensure the medal looks professional and lasts for years.

### Are zinc alloy medals suitable for large events?
Yes, zinc alloy medals are durable, lightweight, and cost-effective for bulk orders, making them ideal for large marathons. The die-casting process efficiently produces thousands of identical medals, and the material's natural corrosion resistance means medals can be stored safely between events.
