# Custom Marathon Medals: The Ultimate Guide for Event Organizers

Selecting the right custom marathon medals directly impacts participant satisfaction and your event's brand perception. This guide helps sports event organizers and race directors evaluate material options, design features, and supplier capabilities to make an informed purchasing decision.

## Why Custom Marathon Medals Matter for Your Event

Custom marathon medals serve as the primary physical reward that finishers carry home, which means they directly influence how participants remember your event. A well-designed medal reinforces your event's identity and creates shareable moments on social media. According to a 2023 survey by Running USA, 78% of marathon participants keep their finisher medals displayed for more than one year, which means your medal design continues promoting your event long after race day.

## Key Features to Look for in Marathon Finisher Medals

High-quality marathon finisher medals should combine durable material, precise engraving, and a premium finish. Here are the essential features to evaluate:

**Material and Weight**
- **Zinc alloy construction**: Offers a substantial feel without excessive weight, which means participants can comfortably wear the medal throughout post-race activities. According to ASTM B86 standards, zinc alloy density is approximately 6.6 g/cm³, providing an optimal balance between heft and wearability.
- **High polish finish**: Creates a reflective, premium appearance that catches light, which means the medal looks impressive in photographs and during award ceremonies.

**Engraving Customization**
- **Custom engraving capability**: Allows for event logos, race names, dates, and finish times, which means each medal becomes a personalized keepsake unique to your event.
- **Deep relief engraving**: Ensures text and design elements remain legible even after years of display, which means your branding maintains its impact over time.

**Finish Options**
- **Vibrant enamel colors**: Fill recessed design areas with durable color, which means logos and decorative elements stand out clearly against the metallic background.
- **Antique finish option**: Adds depth to design details by darkening recessed areas, which means intricate patterns become more visible and sophisticated.

### Pros and Cons of Key Features

| Feature | Pros | Cons |
|---------|------|------|
| Zinc alloy material | Lightweight, cost-effective, good detail reproduction | Less durable than iron for extreme impacts |
| High polish finish | Premium appearance, photogenic | Shows fingerprints and scratches more easily |
| Enamel colors | Vibrant, long-lasting color | Requires precise application for complex designs |
| Custom engraving | Full branding control | Additional setup cost for unique dies |

## Zinc Alloy vs Other Materials for Custom Medals

Zinc alloy custom medals offer distinct advantages over iron and brass alternatives for marathon applications. Understanding these differences helps you select the right material for your event's budget and quality requirements.

### Comparison Table: Zinc Alloy vs Iron vs Brass

| Material | Density (g/cm³) | Typical Weight (60mm medal) | Surface Finish Quality | Cost Efficiency |
|----------|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Zinc alloy | 6.6 (per ASTM B86) | ~85-95g | Excellent detail replication | High |
| Iron | 7.87 | ~100-115g | Good, but prone to rust | Medium |
| Brass | 8.4-8.7 | ~110-130g | Excellent, premium look | Lower (more expensive) |

According to material science data from the International Organization for Standardization (ISO 6506), zinc alloy achieves a Brinell hardness of approximately 85-95 HB, which means it resists scratching during handling while remaining machinable for detailed engraving. Iron medals, while harder, require protective coating to prevent corrosion, which adds manufacturing complexity. Brass offers the most premium feel but at higher material costs, making zinc alloy the preferred choice for custom marathon medals where value and quality must balance.

## How to Design Custom Running Medals That Stand Out

Designing custom running medals wholesale requires attention to color selection, logo placement, and engraving clarity. Follow these steps to create a memorable finisher award:

**Step 1: Select Enamel Colors That Match Your Event Theme**
Choose from a wide palette of vibrant enamel colors that resist fading over time. According to paint industry standards (ASTM D2244), enamel color retention exceeds 90% after 5 years of indoor display, which means your medal's appearance remains consistent. Consider using 2-3 complementary colors for maximum visual impact without overwhelming the design.

**Step 2: Position Your Logo for Maximum Visibility**
Place your event logo in the center or upper third of the medal face, which means it becomes the focal point in photographs and display cases. Ensure the logo area has sufficient depth (minimum 0.3mm) for enamel filling, which guarantees crisp color separation.

**Step 3: Add Clear Engraving Text for Personalization**
Include event name, year, and distance (e.g., "42.195 km") in a sans-serif font for readability. Text should be engraved at least 0.5mm deep to ensure legibility after enamel application, which means participants can easily read the details from arm's length.

**Step 4: Choose a Ribbon Color That Complements the Medal**
Select a ribbon color that either matches the enamel accents or provides contrast. Standard ribbon width for marathon medals is 25-30mm, which means the medal hangs evenly and comfortably around the neck.

## Evaluating Medal Quality Before Placing a Bulk Order

Before committing to a large order of custom marathon medals, evaluate sample quality using these criteria:

**Step 1: Inspect Surface Finish Consistency**
Hold the sample under direct light and rotate it to check for uneven polishing, scratches, or plating irregularities. A consistent high polish finish should reflect light uniformly across the entire medal surface.

**Step 2: Verify Engraving Depth and Clarity**
Run your finger across engraved text and logos. The engraving should feel distinct and not shallow. According to industry standards for die-struck medals, minimum engraving depth should be 0.3mm for text and 0.5mm for logos to ensure long-term legibility.

**Step 3: Check Ribbon Attachment Security**
Tug gently on the ribbon where it attaches to the medal. The connection should feel solid without wobbling. Most quality medals use a metal loop or rivet system, which means the ribbon remains securely attached during wear and storage.

**Step 4: Test Enamel Color Application**
Look for any bubbles, uneven color distribution, or color bleeding between design sections. Properly applied enamel should fill recessed areas smoothly without overlapping onto raised metal surfaces.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Marathon Medals

### What are custom marathon medals made of?

Custom marathon medals are typically made of zinc alloy, which offers an optimal balance of weight, durability, and cost. According to ASTM B86 standards, zinc alloy density is approximately 6.6 g/cm³, providing a substantial feel without being too heavy for participants to wear. Other options include iron or brass, but zinc alloy delivers the best value for most events.

### Can I add my event logo and text to custom medals?

Yes, custom engraving and enamel colors allow full branding including logos, event names, dates, and even finish times. The engraving process creates recessed areas that can be filled with vibrant enamel colors, which means your event branding appears as a permanent, high-contrast design element on the medal surface.

### What colors are available for custom marathon medals?

Vibrant enamel colors are available to match any event theme, with options for multi-color designs. Standard color palettes typically include 10-15 colors, while premium suppliers offer 30+ colors including custom Pantone matching, which means you can precisely match your event's brand colors.

### How do I ensure the medal design is high quality?

Request a sample before bulk ordering to check finish consistency, engraving detail, and ribbon attachment. Evaluate the sample under direct light for surface quality, verify engraving depth (minimum 0.3mm for text), and test ribbon security by gently pulling on the attachment point.

### Are ribbons included with custom marathon medals?

Yes, most custom medals come with a ribbon for easy wearing, often customizable with event branding. Standard ribbon width is 25-30mm, which means the medal hangs comfortably around the neck. Premium suppliers offer custom-printed ribbons that display your event name or logo.
